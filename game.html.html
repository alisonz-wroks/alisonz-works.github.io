Death That Blooms Into a Flower

A narrative-driven interactive game about love, faith, grief, and the ways human beings seek meaning in unbearable circumstances.

Creative Motivation

I created Death That Blooms Into a Flower after becoming deeply interested in how people make meaning when faced with death, religion, and emotional loss. The story is inspired by philosophical and theological questions about love, sacrifice, and human dignity, especially in situations where suffering feels unavoidable.

Rather than telling a linear story, I wanted players to actively participate in the characters’ emotional journeys, allowing their choices to shape how grief, faith, and intimacy are expressed.

Script Development from concept to narrative structure

I began with a high-level narrative outline focused on emotional arcs rather than plot events. Each chapter was designed around a specific emotional state: hope, doubt, despair, and reconciliation.

  • Story outline and branching structure
  • Character backstories and symbolic motifs
  • Dialogue drafts and emotional beats
  • Iterative rewrites based on pacing and player experience
Team Collaboration how I coordinated a multi-disciplinary team

I served as the writer and producer, coordinating between the programmer and the visual artist. My role was to ensure that the narrative, visual tone, and gameplay systems supported the same emotional goals.

  • Created narrative documents and art briefs
  • Provided structured feedback on visual assets
  • Maintained version control and milestone tracking
  • Aligned story changes with technical constraints
Player Feedback how players shaped later revisions

After releasing early builds and the Steam wishlist page, I collected player responses from social media and testers. Many players commented on the emotional weight of the story and the impact of their choices.

  • Adjusted dialogue pacing based on player confusion
  • Refined emotional transitions between scenes
  • Improved clarity of choice consequences
Player-Choice & Emotional Model the computational system behind the story

To move beyond fixed dialogue trees, I designed a system that tracks players’ choices as emotional variables. These variables influence how characters speak, react, and interpret events.

  • Each choice modifies emotional states such as grief, trust, and detachment
  • Dialogue lines are selected based on the current emotional profile
  • The narrative becomes a dynamic response to player behavior

This model allows the game to simulate how real people express themselves differently under emotional pressure, making the story more psychologically realistic.
